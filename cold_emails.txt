WHAT TO AVOID (makes emails sound like AI or corporate)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

✗  "I hope this email finds you well"
✗  "I am reaching out because I am deeply passionate about..."
✗  "I would love the opportunity to leverage my skills"
✗  "I believe I would be a great fit for your organization"
✗  "Please find my resume attached for your consideration"
✗  Long paragraphs — keep each one to 2-3 sentences max
✗  Listing every skill you have — pick the one most relevant to THEM
✗  Asking for a job in the first sentence — build a small connection first
✗  Generic compliments — "I love what you're building" without specifics

✓  Use a specific detail about the company that only someone paying attention would know
✓  Lead with something you DID, not something you ARE
✓  Ask for a call or coffee, not a job
✓  Keep total email under 150 words — shorter is almost always better
✓  End with your name only — signature blocks are for large companies
